Deep Learning Tasks

During the module, I completed several deep learning tasks that helped build my knowledge and understanding of key concepts. These tasks included:

  • Simple Perceptron: Understanding the fundamentals of a single-layer perceptron and its role in binary classification.
  • Perceptron AND Operator: Implementing a perceptron to replicate the behavior of the logical AND operator, reinforcing the basics of neural networks.
  • Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP): Exploring more complex architectures with multiple layers to solve non-linear problems.
  • Gradient Descent and Cost Function: Learning how gradient descent optimizes neural networks by minimizing the cost function.
  • Running a CNN Model for Object Recognition: Working with prebuilt Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to understand their structure and application to object recognition tasks.
  • Creating My Own CNN Model: Designing and implementing a custom CNN architecture to solve a specific image classification problem.
  • Model Performance Measurement: Evaluating model accuracy, loss, and other metrics to assess the effectiveness of deep learning models.

These tasks provided hands-on experience with the fundamental principles and practical applications of deep learning.
